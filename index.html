How It Works

Three steps to reading

1

Point your phone at a book

Hold your iPhone over any page. TapNRead's camera instantly detects every word and places gentle highlights over each one. The boxes stay perfectly still — no jittering.

2

Your child taps a word

Big, easy-to-tap buttons sized for little fingers. Tap a word to hear it spoken aloud. A satisfying sound and gentle vibration confirm every tap.

3

Practice and sound it out

Turn on Practice Mode and your child tries saying the word first. Stars celebrate success! The "Sound it out" button breaks tough words into syllables for step-by-step help.

Support

Frequently asked questions

What age is TapNRead for?

TapNRead is designed for pre-K and kindergarten children, typically ages 3-6. It works great for any early reader building confidence with words.

Does it work with any book?

Yes! TapNRead works with any printed text — children's books, library books, worksheets, even signs. If it has words, TapNRead can read them. It works best with clear text and good lighting.

Does it need internet?

No. Everything runs on your iPhone — text recognition, speech, and pronunciation checking all work offline. No data is sent anywhere.

Is my child's data safe?

Absolutely. TapNRead collects zero data. No accounts, no tracking, no analytics. Camera and microphone are processed entirely on your device. See our full privacy policy.

How do I turn on Pronunciation Practice?

Open Settings (gear icon in the top-right corner) and toggle "Pronunciation Practice" on. The app will ask for microphone and speech recognition permission the first time.

What does "Sound it out" do?

It breaks a word into syllables (like EL-E-PHANT) and plays each part one at a time, then says the whole word. It's how teachers help kids decode unfamiliar words.

What if the words aren't detected correctly?

Make sure you have good lighting on the book page. Hold the phone 8-12 inches above the page. If words look wrong, shake the phone to rescan. The flashlight button can help in dim rooms.

Can I adjust the voice speed?

Yes! In Settings you can adjust speech speed (slower is better for young learners) and voice pitch (higher pitch sounds friendlier for kids). You can also choose between male and female voices.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

TapNRead ("the App") is developed by Pingalaa LLC. This privacy policy explains how we handle information in connection with the App.

Information We Collect

TapNRead does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. The App runs entirely on your device.

Camera Access

The App uses your device's camera to detect text in books and printed materials. Camera frames are processed on-device in real-time using Apple's Vision framework and are never recorded, stored, or transmitted to any server.

Microphone Access

When Pronunciation Practice mode is enabled, the App uses the microphone to listen for your child's speech. Audio is processed on-device using Apple's Speech framework for pronunciation checking. Audio is never recorded, stored, or transmitted.

Speech Recognition

Speech recognition is performed on-device using Apple's built-in speech recognition technology. The App requests on-device processing to ensure audio data stays on your device.

Data Storage

TapNRead does not store any reading data, history, or progress information. Each session starts fresh. No data persists between app uses.

Third-Party Services

TapNRead does not use any third-party services, analytics, advertising, or tracking tools.

Children's Privacy

TapNRead is designed for children ages 3-6. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We do not knowingly collect any information from children or any users. There is no information to collect — the App processes everything locally on the device.

Internet Access

TapNRead does not require or use an internet connection. The App functions entirely offline.

Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the App constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
